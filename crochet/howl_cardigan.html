Howl Pendragon's Cardigan
(from Howl's Moving Castle, a movie adaptation by Studio Ghibli)
I learned to crochet specially for this project! It was all leading up to this.


I am glad I practiced with smaller projects because this was definitely my most difficult crochet project. I cannot count how many times I unraveled a section because I made a mistake or because I was unsatisfied with how it looked. There was a lot of trial and error for the sleeves since I strayed from the pattern.
Because I am a noob and I do not understand test swatches, I have a lot of left-over skeins and the back piece was very wide. Meanwhile, I had a hard time stretching the pieces length-wise to the given measurements in the pattern. Before assembling the parts, I blocked them to get them to the right measurements.
I am impressed with myself for being able to follow a written pattern and for finishing the entire thing! I don't want to touch yarn again anytime soon though.

Pattern: The Starboy Coat by Mela Gonzalez (@reclamare.ph on instagram)
Pattern modifications:
I crocheted the sleeves directly from the arm holes instead of starting from the cuffs because I wanted the sleeves to be more wide and oversized like in the movie. This also allowed me to control the length of the sleeves and cuffs so they could fit me. I used a row of double crochet? instead of single crochet for the yellow borders on the cuffs. I crocheted the yellow trim (double crochet?) all around the cardigan because it made more sense to my sewing brain (if the cuffs, the collar and the edges are hemmed with yellow, why not the bottom edge too?).
If I figure out a way to do it in the future, I might add a red lining inside the cardigan for even more accuracy.

Materials:
21 skeins of Bernat Handicrafter 100% Cotton yarn, 50g (French blue: 10, Off white: 1, Tangerine: 10)
1 skein of Lily Sugar'n Cream Cotton yarn (Yellow)
4.5 mm hook
Safety pins
Towel, pins and weights or heavy objects (for wet-blocking)
